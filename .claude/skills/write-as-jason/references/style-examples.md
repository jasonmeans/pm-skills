Extracted from Jason's Medium blog posts to serve as reference material for voice matching.

## Opening Hooks (Personal Anecdotes)

### From "Focus Your Focus!"
> Over the past 15 years, I found myself deeply immersed in the World of Warcraft, raiding with a guild of friends and coworkers. Our raid leader had a battle cry that became legendary among us: "Focus your focus!" This phrase was more than just a catchy motivator; it was a battle cry during those grueling nights when we were wiping on the same boss over and over again.

### From "Layoff Advice for Friends"
> In 1995 I was walking home from school and found a Zenith 8086 in my neighbor's trash. Their family got a fancy new Windows 95 machine. We did not have a PC in our home, so I brought the Zenith into our basement. I spent hours in front of that glowing green LCD screen. Booting from 5 1/4" floppies and reading reference manuals on BASIC.

Every day my Dad commuted to his job as a locksmith off the island while my Mom stayed home to raise me and my sister. We did not have a lot of things, and what we did have, we had to work hard to earn them.

### From "The Power of Question-Storming"
> In 2016 I joined a new team and took on a new problem space. Unfortunately, as I was building credibility with the team, I managed to lead one of my career's most unsuccessful brainstorming sessions.

> I got desperate. One evening I decided to lock my computer and leave at 5PM, blowing a major deadline with a big customer just to prove I was done working over 20 hours straight due to poor planning. You can imagine how that went the next morning.

However, if you are only down by one and have no outs, a popup to the outfield will work.

## Frameworks and Practical Advice

### Numbered principles (from "Focus Your Focus!")
> 1. **Doing Fewer Things at the Same Time:** In a world that often celebrates multitasking, I am come to value the power of single-tasking.
> 2. **Taking Breaks and Working Naturally:** Our brains are not built for sustained focus without pause.
> 3. **Obsessing About Quality:** Quality over quantity has become a mantra for me.

### The 3Ps framework (from "Wildfires and Careers")
> 1. Profession: Does the job require you to do work that you enjoy?
> 2. Product: Is the product that you are working on something that excites you?
> 3. People: Are you working with great people?

### Three questions before committing (from "Focus Your Focus!")
> 1. **Does this align with my core values?**
> 2. **Will this make a meaningful difference?**
> 3. **Is this the best use of my time and energy?**

## Closing Patterns

### Call to action (from "Focus Your Focus!")
> Over the next 30 days, I encourage you to try a few changes to help you focus on what truly matters and reclaim control over your productivity.

### Warm callback (from "Work-Life Balance")
> I can say with a straight face now, Tony, that I have a great work-life balance. [...] Then again, sometimes we just need to play less World of Warcraft.

### Engagement questions (from "Work-Life Balance")
> - How do you measure your work-life balance? Any tips?
> - Any suggestions for people struggling with work-life balance?
> - What holds you back from saying "no" to things?

### Brief sign-off (from "Question-Storming")
> Keep growing, my friends.

## Core Values (from "Focus Your Focus!")
> 1. **Always give my best effort:** Whether solving complex problems or learning something new, always do my best to deliver high-quality.
> 2. **Be a good person:** Kindness and empathy in every interaction foster trust and meaningful relationships.
> 3. **Help others:** Supporting someone new on the team or helping with challenges brings fulfillment and creates a positive impact.
> 4. **End each week with gratitude:** Reflecting on wins and thanking someone grounds me and strengthens my connections.
