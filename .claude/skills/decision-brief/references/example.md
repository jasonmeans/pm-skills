# Decision Brief: Vendor Selection for Monterey Retail Site

## Introduction

Good morning. The purpose of my briefing today is to obtain a decision from you regarding which vendor to select for the quality inspection system at our new Monterey retail site. I'll share the situation, lay out our options, compare them against criteria, give my recommendation, and then ask for your decision.

## Situation

- Our current quality inspection system produces a 3% defect pass-through rate
- Customer tolerance is 0.03% -- we are 100x above acceptable levels
- We have received two formal complaints from key accounts in the past quarter
- The new Monterey site is scheduled to open in Q3 and requires a quality system at launch
- Budget authority for this purchase sits with this group

## Options

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Option A: VendorAlpha Pro** | Cloud-based inspection platform with AI-assisted defect detection. 3-year contract. |
| **Option B: VendorBeta Standard** | On-premise inspection system with manual calibration. 1-year renewable license. |
| **Option C: Status Quo** | Continue using the existing legacy inspection system at the new site. |

## Comparison

### Criteria

1. **Cost** -- Total cost of ownership over 3 years including licensing, hardware, and maintenance
2. **Defect Detection Accuracy** -- Percentage of defective parts correctly identified under standard operating conditions
3. **Implementation Timeline** -- Time from contract signing to fully operational at the Monterey site
4. **Vendor Support** -- Availability of on-site support, SLA response times, and training included in contract
5. **Scalability** -- Ability to expand to additional sites without significant re-investment

### Comparison Table

| Criteria | VendorAlpha Pro | VendorBeta Standard | Status Quo |
|----------|----------------|--------------------:|------------|
| **Cost (3-yr TCO)** | $420K | $285K | $90K (maintenance only) |
| **Defect Detection** | 99.97% accuracy | 99.8% accuracy | 97% accuracy |
| **Implementation** | 10 weeks | 6 weeks | No change needed |
| **Vendor Support** | 24/7 SLA, on-site training included | Business hours SLA, training extra ($15K) | No vendor support, internal only |
| **Scalability** | Add sites via license, no new hardware | Requires new hardware per site ($80K each) | Not viable at scale |

### Time and Risk Considerations

- **Time:** Every week of delay past Q3 opening costs approximately $35K in lost revenue. VendorAlpha's 10-week timeline leaves 2 weeks of buffer. VendorBeta's 6-week timeline leaves 6 weeks of buffer.
- **Risk (VendorAlpha):** Cloud dependency means outage risk; mitigated by offline fallback mode included in contract
- **Risk (VendorBeta):** Manual calibration introduces human error; requires dedicated trained operator on-site
- **Risk (Status Quo):** Customer complaint escalation is likely. Losing one key account would exceed the cost difference between all three options.

## Recommendation

Based on this comparison and the critical gap between our current 3% defect rate and the 0.03% customer expectation, my recommendation is to select VendorAlpha Pro. Its detection accuracy meets customer standards, and its scalability positions us for future site expansions without repeated capital investment.

## Request

I've recommended VendorAlpha Pro. What's your decision?
